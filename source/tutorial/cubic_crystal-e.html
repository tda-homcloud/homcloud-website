Analysis of crystal data, especially volume-optimal cycle and tightened-volume

In this article we will analyze the data of a 3x3 cubic grid. Analyze noise-added data instead of a perfect cubic grid. Here, we analyze the first-order persistent diagram.

First, calculate the persistent diagram.

In[1]:
python3 -m homcloud.pc_alpha -D -I -d 3 --no-square cubic_crystal.txt cubic_crystal.idiagram
In[2]:
python3 -m homcloud.plot_PD -d 1 -x 0.4:0.75 -l cubic_crystal.idiagram -o cubic_crystal_PD1.png
display < cubic_crystal_PD1.png




































Points are concentrated around birth 0.5, death 0.7. This corresponds to a ring made by a square with a side length of 1 in a cubic lattice.


Birth corresponds to 1/2 of 1 = 0.5, and death corresponds to 1/2 of $\sqrt{2}$ (diagonal length of square) = 0.70.. .
The birth-death pair spreads as much as the noise is added.

You can see that a rectangular shape consisting of 6 vertices is calculated.It is said that this birth-death pair corresponds to a square with a side length of 1, but in this case a rectangle is obtained. In fact, persistent homology often shows multiple unit structures attached to each other.


Here, how "reliable" is this rectangle? To sum up, how robust is it to noise? Really, this kind of thing "with multiple units"  are unstable against noise.


Then, is there something "more stable against noise"? HomCloud implements a tightened volume, and that can be used.






















So, Let's calculate the tightened volume. You can calculate this by giving homcloud.optimal_volume the option --tightened-volume THRESHOLD.


















In[9]:






# 
python3 -m homcloud.optimal_volume -d 1 -x 0.5091621008021502 -y 0.6975775524547941 --tightened-volume 0.05 \
  --no-optimal-volume on -P cubic_crystal.idiagram

jq -r '.result[0]."tightened-volume"."boundary-points"[] | @tsv ' < tv-2.json

Note
The optimal volume is the area inside the volume-optimal cycle.
What we are actually calculating is the optimal volume and the volume-optimal cycle is at the boundary.


An argument of --tightened-volume, 0.05 means "examine stable information even with about this much noise added". Here, the value is determined based on the distribution range of the birth-death pair near (0.5, 0.7).


At present, there is no mathmatical guarantee that the tightened volume used here really sees "stable information". Perhaps some mathematical characterization is possible, but that is for the future.


In addition to --tightened-volume, there are similar functions such as --tightened-subvolume and --owned-volume.
Although the calculation algorithm is different, the results obtained are similar. These use the calculation results of the volume-optimal cycle, so remove -no-optimal-volume on.
